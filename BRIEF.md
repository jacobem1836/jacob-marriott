The site is a hub. Two subpages serve specific audiences; the main page works for everyone.

### Site structure

```
jacobmarriott.com          – main: who you are, your work, contact
jacobmarriott.com/consult  – AI systems & consulting (for prospective consulting clients)
jacobmarriott.com/web      – web dev for hospitality (links out or describes web.jacobmarriott.com work)
```

The main page should not try to sell consulting or web dev directly – it establishes Jacob as a credible builder who does real work. The subpages give each audience a focused landing point.

## Voice & Tone

**Adjectives:** warm · direct · curious · adventurous · grounded

**Register:** conversational but professional. Reads like someone talking, not a corporate bio.

### Signature moves
- **Em dashes** – used constantly to insert colour mid-sentence
- **Parenthetical asides with personality**
- **Narrative over bullet points** – connects disparate experiences into a thread
- **Enthusiasm without boasting**
- **Confident but accessible** – states what he brings, no puffery
- **First person throughout**

### Avoid
- Stiff corporate-speak
- Safe, generic portfolio copy
- Fake humility or over-claiming
- Tech-bro buzzword density

---

## Design Constraints

**Do not redesign the existing site.** The current aesthetic is intentional and should be preserved:
- Typography-led, minimal, editorial
- Monospace section labels (`01 / intro`, `03 / work`)
- Muted color palette with a single accent
- Clean spacing, border-top dividers between work items
- Blinking cursor in hero
- Responsive two-column work layout on desktop

Changes should feel like natural extensions of what's already there – not a retheme.
